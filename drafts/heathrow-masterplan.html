The masterplan
case.title
More than just a runway
case.prose

To operate a three-runway airport, we also need to build passenger facilities, infrastructure and relocate or replace some existing buildings and land uses. Our Preferred Masterplan sets out our plans for the future expansion of Heathrow.

The masterplan has been developed taking into account feedback from Airport Expansion Consultation One in January 2018, as well as community, consumer and stakeholder engagement events and ongoing design work. It is designed around four principles: growing responsibly within environmental limits, providing world-class surface access, creating a great place to work and visit, and protecting and enhancing the local environment.

21 components of the masterplan
sections.lead
Our Preferred Masterplan sets out every element required to operate a world-class three-runway airport — from the new runway itself to a green loop, tunnelled motorway and community facilities.
card-1.num
01 — New Northwest Runway
card-1.title
A new runway for the UK
card-1.body
The new northwest runway is the centrepiece of the masterplan — the first new runway in the south-east of England in over 60 years, enabling 260,000 additional flights per year.
card-2.num
02 — Terminal Facilities
card-2.title
World-class passenger experience
card-2.body
New and expanded terminal facilities to handle 142 million passengers per year, with improved connections between all terminals via the expanded Central Terminal Area.
card-3.num
03 — M25 Tunnel
card-3.title
Tunnelling under the motorway
card-3.body
The M25 will be diverted into a new tunnel beneath the airport boundary, removing a major constraint on the runway layout and improving surface access to the expanded airport.
card-4.num
04 — Green Loop
card-4.title
A new green space for the community
card-4.body
A continuous landscape corridor encircling the expanded airport, providing public open space, biodiversity net gain and a buffer between the airport and surrounding communities.
