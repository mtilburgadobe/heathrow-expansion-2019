case.eyebrow-left
The case for expansion
case.title
The UK's hub airport is full
case.prose

Heathrow is the UK's only hub airport and has been operating at 98% of its capacity since 2005. This constrains competition between airlines, limits new routes and keeps fares higher than they should be.

The Airports Commission concluded in 2015 that not expanding capacity in the south-east would be damaging to the UK economy and to the UK's hub status. Parliament approved the Airports National Policy Statement (NPS) designating a new northwest runway at Heathrow in June 2018.

A new northwest runway is the centrepiece of our plans, but this is about much more. It is about transforming Heathrow into a world-class, sustainable hub airport that works for passengers, colleagues, local communities and the wider UK.

Heathrow currently serves around 80 airlines flying to 185 destinations. Expansion will open new long-haul routes, strengthen the UK's trade connections post-Brexit, and support 114,000 new jobs — not just at the airport, but across the supply chain and wider economy.
