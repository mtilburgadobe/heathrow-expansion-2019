What the masterplan covers

More than just a runway

To operate a three-runway airport, we also need to build passenger facilities, infrastructure and relocate or replace some existing buildings and land uses. Our Preferred Masterplan sets out our plans for the future expansion of Heathrow.

The masterplan has been developed taking into account feedback from Airport Expansion Consultation One in January 2018, as well as community, consumer and stakeholder engagement events and ongoing design and assessment work.

The boundary of an expanded Heathrow will be carefully designed to improve the overall appearance of the airport and help it blend into the surrounding area.

The proposed northwest runway will be located to the north of the existing airport and will be approximately 3,500m long. The new runway would not be open before 2026, which is 11 years after the Airports Commission released its final report.

The Preferred Masterplan at a glance

The expanded airport will be capable of handling up to 142 million passengers per annum at full build-out (Phase 4), compared to 80 million today. A new northwest runway will deliver at least 260,000 additional Air Transport Movements per year.

The masterplan includes a new Central Terminal Area, a tunnelled M25, an expanded road network, and a 20km Green Loop around the airport perimeter connecting communities, open spaces and walking and cycling routes.

21 masterplan components

The masterplan sets out 21 distinct components covering the new runway, terminal buildings, surface access, roads and tunnels, utilities, employment and logistics areas, and environmental infrastructure including river realignments and the Green Loop.

The 21 components

What we are proposing to build

The Preferred Masterplan comprises 21 separate components spanning airfield infrastructure, passenger terminals, surface access, communities and the environment.

Airfield

New Northwest Runway

A new 3,500m runway to the northwest of the existing airport. The runway will not open before 2026. M25 will be placed in a tunnel beneath it.

Airfield

New Taxiways and Aprons

A network of new taxiways and aircraft stands to serve the additional runway and expanded terminal facilities.

Terminals

New Terminal Facilities

New and expanded terminal buildings to provide the additional passenger capacity needed as the airport grows through its four phases.

Terminals

Expanded Central Terminal Area

Redevelopment and expansion of the existing central terminal area including Terminals 2, 3, 4 and 5 to serve a growing passenger base.

Surface Access

M25 Tunnel

The M25 motorway will be placed in a tunnel under the new northwest runway. This is a major piece of civil engineering infrastructure.

Surface Access

New Road Network

Diversion of existing roads, construction of new local roads and connections, and new junctions to serve the expanded airport.

Surface Access

Southern Access Tunnel

A proposed new tunnel providing southern access to the Central Terminal Area, transforming Heathrow's accessibility from south London and Surrey.

Surface Access

Rail and Public Transport

Enhanced rail connections including Elizabeth Line (Crossrail), Western Rail Link, Southern Rail Access and expanded bus and coach services.

Environment

Green Loop

A 20km walking and cycling loop around the airport perimeter connecting communities, open spaces and biodiversity sites to public spaces and paths.

Environment

River Realignments

Diversion of the River Colne, Wraysbury River, Longford River and other watercourses in the Colne Valley to accommodate the new runway footprint.

Environment

Flood Storage Areas

New flood storage areas upstream of the airport, designed as multi-functional spaces for biodiversity and public open space.

Communities

Harmondsworth Conservation Area

The new runway has been carefully positioned to retain the historic High Street and the Harmondsworth conservation area. A new Visitor Centre is proposed around the Great Barn and St Mary's Church.

Communities

Employment Sites

New and relocated employment sites, including logistics and cargo facilities, positioned close to public transport hubs to reduce car travel for colleagues.

Communities

Community Facilities

Replacement of community facilities including sports pitches, allotments and public open spaces displaced by construction.

Utilities

Utilities and Energy

New utility infrastructure including electricity, gas, water and sewerage to serve the expanded airport campus, including provision for plug-in electrical power for parked aircraft.

Freight

Cargo and Freight

Doubled cargo capacity with new off-airport waiting areas for HGVs and improved logistics management to reduce freight vehicles on local roads.

Parking

Car Parking Consolidation

Consolidation of car parking at 'parkways' around the airport perimeter with high-frequency shuttle links to terminals, reducing on-site parking per passenger.

Airfield

Noise Screens and Barriers

Displaced landing thresholds and noise barriers embedded in the airport design to reduce ground noise for communities closest to the airport boundary.

Environment

Biodiversity Net Gain

Large areas of multi-functional green infrastructure designed to benefit both wildlife and people. Commitment to achieve net gain in biodiversity.

Buildings

Relocated Buildings and Land Uses

Some existing buildings and community facilities will need to be moved, including the Immigration Removal Centres at Harmondsworth and Colnbrook.

Environment

Landscape and Visual Design

A Landscape Strategy and Landscape Toolkit to ensure the expanded airport blends into its surroundings, using ecological woodland and biodiverse grassland.

Construction timeline

Four phases of development

The construction and opening of Heathrow will be delivered in four phases spanning from 2021 to approximately 2050, growing capacity from today's 80 million passengers to 142 million at full build-out.

Phase 1 — Early Works

Approximately 2021–2026
~80mppa
Capacity (existing)
  • M25 diversion and tunnel construction
  • River diversions in the Colne Valley
  • Initial earthworks and site preparation
  • Taxiway and apron construction begins
  • Surface access improvements begin

Phase 2 — Runway Opens

Approximately 2026–2030
115mppa
Target capacity
  • New northwest runway opens (target ~2026)
  • New terminal facilities operational
  • Southern access tunnel opens
  • Elizabeth Line and Western Rail Link serving airport
  • Night flight ban (23:00–05:30) in effect from opening

Phase 3 — Additional Capacity

Approximately 2030–2035
130mppa
Target capacity
  • Further terminal expansion
  • Additional airfield infrastructure
  • Expanded Central Terminal Area
  • Further surface access improvements
  • Green Loop fully open to public

Phase 4 — Full Build-Out

Approximately 2035–2050
142mppa
Full build-out capacity
  • All terminal and airfield facilities complete
  • Full cargo capacity (doubled from today)
  • Complete surface access network operational
  • All environmental mitigation measures in place
  • Zero carbon infrastructure by 2050

Key infrastructure proposals

Making Heathrow fit for the future

The masterplan is designed around four principles: growing responsibly within environmental limits, providing world-class surface access, creating a great place to work and visit, and protecting and enhancing the local environment.

Surface access — no more airport-related traffic

We have pledged to deliver expansion with no more airport-related traffic on the roads than there is today. Our Surface Access Proposals cover seven areas:

  • Public transport — 50% of passenger trips by public transport by 2030, 55% by 2040
  • Colleague travel — reduce colleague car trips by 25% by 2030, 50% by 2040
  • Road user charging — Heathrow Ultra-Low Emission Zone (HULEZ) from 2022; Heathrow Vehicle Access Charge (HVAC) from runway opening
  • Intelligent mobility — Heathrow Travel Wallet, personalised journey information services
  • Car parking — consolidated parkways; increase in EV charging provision
  • Freight — off-airport HGV holding areas; doubled cargo capacity
  • Taxi and private hire — increased backfilling to reduce empty vehicles

The Green Loop

A key feature of the masterplan is the planned Green Loop — a 20km walking and cycling route around the airport perimeter. The Green Loop will:

  • Improve connectivity around the airport and into nearby communities
  • Link landscaped areas, public open spaces and biodiversity sites
  • Connect historic places including Harmondsworth village and the Great Barn
  • Provide new walking and cycling routes between communities and parkways

Harmondsworth village

Following feedback from Airport Expansion Consultation One, we have reduced the amount of development in the Mayfield Farm area. The proposed runway has been carefully positioned to retain the historic High Street and the most significant built heritage assets — including the Harmondsworth conservation area and the medieval Great Barn.
