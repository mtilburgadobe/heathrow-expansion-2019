case.eyebrow-left
Runway alternation
case.title
Respite for every community
case.prose

During westerly operations we run a two-week alternation cycle, switching runways at 3pm each day. Every week communities under both runway paths get predictable respite from aircraft noise for half the day.

With three runways we have four possible mode allocations in a repeating four-day sequence. We are consulting on whether the daily switchover should happen at 14:00 or 15:00. Whichever communities are under the northern or southern runway get a full day free of overhead aircraft. The central runway operates in mixed mode — arrivals and departures — throughout.

How the airport will work

Four operational priorities
sections.lead
Expansion is a unique opportunity to change how people and goods travel to, from and around the airport — and how the airport itself operates to minimise its impact on communities.
card-1.num
01 — Runway Alternation
card-1.title
Predictable noise respite
card-1.body
A two-week alternation cycle means every community under a runway path gets half the day free from aircraft noise. Four mode allocations rotate over four days, sharing the noise fairly.
card-2.num
02 — Night Flights
card-2.title
A 6.5-hour ban on scheduled flights
card-2.body
No scheduled flights between 23:00 and 05:30 — the longest scheduled ban at any hub airport in Europe. Early morning arrivals use a single runway so each community gets at least 7 hours' respite overnight.
card-3.num
03 — Early Growth
card-3.title
More flights before the runway opens
card-3.body
Before the new runway opens we propose to bring up to 25,000 additional Air Transport Movements per year forward on the existing two runways, within a strict noise envelope.
card-4.num
04 — Surface Access
card-4.title
No more airport-related traffic
card-4.body
We are committed to delivering expansion with no more airport-related road traffic than today, and at least 50% of passenger trips by public transport by 2030, through improved rail, bus and active travel options.
