Environment & Community

Developing Heathrow responsibly and sustainably

Expansion is not a choice between the economy and the environment — it must deliver for both. Our environmental framework commits us to grow only within strict limits monitored by an independent body.

Section 7

Managing the effects of expansion

We have carried out a Preliminary Environmental Information Report (PEIR) assessing the likely significant environmental effects of the project. The table below summarises the key topics. We are carefully considering measures to avoid, reduce or offset any significant adverse effects wherever possible.

Air quality & odour

Surface access proposals will minimise vehicle emissions. Construction activities will use best-practice dust control. Air quality is expected to improve during operation as road vehicles and aircraft become cleaner.

Noise

A 6.5-hour ban on scheduled night flights, runway alternation giving predictable daytime respite, a legally-binding noise envelope, and three noise insulation schemes for eligible properties.

Community & health

756 homes required within the Compulsory Purchase Zone. A Home Relocation Support Service, Community Safety Management Plan, and Community Fund will help manage and mitigate impacts.

Biodiversity

We are committed to achieving a net gain in biodiversity — more wildlife habitat after expansion than before. New multi-functional green infrastructure across the site will benefit both wildlife and people.

Historic environment

Expansion presents challenges but also opportunities. The proposed runway has been carefully positioned to retain the historic High Street and the most significant heritage assets. Four historic environment strategies will address effects.

Water environment

Watercourses including the River Colne, Wraysbury River and Longford River will be diverted. New flood storage areas will be multifunctional spaces for biodiversity and public open space.

Carbon

Our long-term aspiration is to make growth from the new runway carbon neutral. We are committed to zero-carbon infrastructure — buildings and other fixed assets — by 2050. Growth in flight emissions will be largely offset through CORSIA.

Landscape & visual

A Landscape Toolkit sets out guidelines to achieve our emerging Landscape Strategy. A planned 20km Green Loop will link communities, parks, biodiversity sites and cycling routes around the perimeter.

Socio-economics

Over 70,000 people work on-site across 400+ businesses. Expansion provides new employment and training, including a commitment to 10,000 apprenticeships and 1,200 T-level work-placement days from September 2020.

Section 7.4

Noise management

Noise is one of the most important issues for communities around Heathrow. Despite a significant increase in flights over decades, our noise contour footprint has shrunk considerably. We want to take the opportunities presented by expansion to further improve how we manage noise.

Our noise management approach is aligned with the ICAO Balanced Approach to Aircraft Noise Management: incentivising quieter aircraft, quieter operating procedures (steeper approaches, displaced landing thresholds), operational limits on noisier aircraft, and compensatory insulation, before any measures that restrict airline operations.

Our updated noise objective is: "To limit and, where possible, reduce the effects of noise on health and quality of life and deliver regular breaks from scheduled flights for our communities during the day and night."

The noise envelope is a set of legally binding and enforceable limits and controls on noise that will allow the airport to grow while protecting communities. It will include enforceable limits bounding airspace change and Heathrow operations, local priorities, and mechanisms for sharing future technological improvements between airport growth and reducing noise impact on communities.

Three noise insulation schemes

1

Scheme 1 — Aircraft noise

For eligible properties most affected by aircraft noise — a full package of sound insulation to habitable rooms.

2

Scheme 2 — Construction, road and rail

For eligible properties to address noise from construction, road or rail sources, including properties that do not qualify under Scheme 1.

3

Scheme 3 — £3,000 contribution

A £3,000 contribution to a package of sound insulation treatment for eligible properties in designated noise areas.

We will seek DCO powers to carry out noise insulation works compulsorily for properties at the end of the new runway if they cannot be delivered voluntarily.

Section 7.2

Community impacts

The construction and operation of the project will affect local communities in different ways. Land is needed for the new runway, terminals, aprons and supporting infrastructure, and some homes, community facilities and open spaces will be affected.

The construction of the project will require the acquisition of 756 homes, displacing residents — a significant negative effect. We have planned a number of measures to avoid, reduce, mitigate or compensate for these effects:

  • Replacement of community facilities, public open space (including sports facilities and allotments) and recreational routes (including public rights of way)
  • Compensation for home-owners in the Compulsory Purchase Zone and Wider Property Offer Zone
  • A Home Relocation Support Service to guide owners and residents through the DCO application and relocation process
  • An Independent Resident Advice Service
  • A Construction Workforce Accommodation Strategy to reduce effects on private sector housing
  • Welfare provision and occupational health services within construction sites
  • A Community Fund to improve local quality of life
  • A Community Safety Management Plan

New recreational spaces and routes are predicted as permanent positive effects after the new runway opens.

Section 7.5

Socio-economics & employment

Heathrow is one of the largest single-site employers in the UK. Over 70,000 people work on-site across more than 400 businesses. Expansion will provide significant new employment and training opportunities.

We are committed to:

  • 10,000 apprenticeships at an expanded airport
  • 10,000 work experience days by 2030 for people with additional learning needs, disabilities, older job seekers and groups facing barriers to employment
  • 1,200 T-level work-placement days for local students in construction and digital careers from September 2020
  • A Shared Apprenticeship Scheme and Skills Passport to help apprentices develop transferable skills

The construction of the third runway will result in significant positive effects on the labour market by providing jobs, opportunities for skills enhancement and training, and creating opportunities for businesses in the supply chain.

Section 8

Growing within environmental limits

Expansion at Heathrow is not a choice between the economy and the environment — it must deliver for both. Our Environmentally Managed Growth framework sets legally binding limits on four key environmental metrics, monitored and enforced by an independent Scrutiny Panel.

Surface access

At least 50% of passenger trips by public transport by 2030, rising to 55% by 2040. A 25% reduction in colleague car trips by 2030 and 50% by 2040. No more airport-related traffic on roads than today.

Air quality

Expansion must not cause a compliant air quality zone to become non-compliant. We will minimise air quality levels below the legal limit. An independent Scrutiny Panel will monitor and enforce compliance.

Aircraft noise

Any noise management measures must avoid significant adverse impacts, mitigate impacts on health and quality of life, and where possible contribute to improvements. A noise envelope provides the legally binding framework. There must be a progressive improvement in Heathrow's noise performance over time.

Section 9

Community Fund

As part of our expansion plans, we are proposing a new Community Fund to help address the positive and negative effects of the project and improve the quality of life in the area around the airport.

What could it be spent on?

  • Enhancements to quality of life including community activities, health, transport and environmental improvements
  • Initiatives that help enhance the benefits of expansion for local communities, such as support for the Heathrow Academy, skills and jobs initiatives, and apprenticeship schemes
  • Enhancements of re-provided facilities or services with a wider community benefit (e.g. additional play areas and school places)
  • Additional or ongoing mitigation measures, particularly where it is not possible to identify or quantify measures necessary at the time of DCO application

Eligibility

We would focus eligibility on areas close to construction sites and the airport itself — key roads and public transport routes, and areas within a particular noise contour — to ensure the fund is proportionate to environmental impacts.

Sources of funding

  • Heathrow funding of transport, environmental, community and health measures that meet our guiding principles
  • A passenger levy — similar to the noise levy recommended by the Airports Commission — ensuring those who benefit from expansion contribute directly to measures that address and compensate for negative effects
  • Further funding raised through an airport charge paid by airlines, used for mitigation and compensation measures which enhance quality of life

Duration

The Fund could start during construction or when the new runway opens. Some parts could end when relevant impacts end; other elements could be on-going, managed by a review process. We anticipate the size will vary over time as the project moves through its different phases.

Section 10

Property and compensation

We recognise that people who live in or own property near Heathrow could be affected by expansion. We have developed five interim property policies and discretionary compensation schemes for owners or occupiers of land that may need to be acquired or may be affected by expansion.

1

Residential Property Policy

Covers properties in the Compulsory Purchase Zone (CPZ) and Wider Property Offer Zone (WPOZ).

2

Agricultural Land & Property

Sets out our approach for owners of agricultural land together with farmhouses and associated buildings.

3

Commercial Property

Sets out our approach for larger commercial interests and our offer for those with small business interests.

4

Property Hardship Scheme

For residential, agricultural and small business owners with a compelling need to sell but unable to do so on the open market.

5

Professional Fees

Sets out our approach to professional fees incurred in connection with the compulsory acquisition process.

Compulsory Purchase Zone (CPZ)

The large area of land that will need to be acquired by Heathrow, including residential, commercial and agricultural properties. All five interim property policies apply within the CPZ.

Wider Property Offer Zone (WPOZ)

Properties outside the CPZ but within close proximity to the proposed new boundary that could be affected by activities, even though they are not required for the project. Four policies apply — Residential, Commercial, Professional Fees, and Property Hardship Scheme.
