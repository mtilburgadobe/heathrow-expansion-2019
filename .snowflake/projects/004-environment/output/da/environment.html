case.eyebrow-left
Environmental framework
case.title
Growing within environmental limits
case.prose

Our Environmentally Managed Growth framework sets legally binding limits on four key environmental metrics, monitored and enforced by an independent Scrutiny Panel. Expansion at Heathrow is not a choice between the economy and the environment — it must deliver for both.

We have carried out a Preliminary Environmental Information Report (PEIR) assessing the likely significant environmental effects of the project across nine topic areas: air quality, noise, community and health, biodiversity, historic environment, water environment, carbon, landscape and visual impact, and socio-economics.

case.eyebrow-right
Our commitments
tl-1.year
Surface access
tl-1.text
At least 50% of passenger trips by public transport by 2030, rising to 55% by 2040. No more airport-related traffic on roads than today.
tl-2.year
Air quality
tl-2.text
Expansion must not cause a compliant air quality zone to become non-compliant. We will minimise air quality levels below the legal limit, monitored by an independent Scrutiny Panel.
tl-3.year
Aircraft noise
tl-3.text
Noise management measures must avoid significant adverse impacts and contribute to progressive improvements in noise performance. A noise envelope provides the legally binding framework.
tl-4.year
Biodiversity
tl-4.text
Heathrow is committed to delivering biodiversity net gain — creating more and better habitat than is lost, including through the Green Loop and river realignment schemes.
tl-5.year
Carbon
tl-5.text
Any growth in carbon emissions from the airport must be consistent with the UK's obligations under the Climate Change Act and any future international aviation agreements.
sections.eyebrow
Managing the effects of expansion
sections.title
Four areas of community impact
sections.lead
We are carefully considering measures to avoid, reduce or offset any significant adverse effects of expansion wherever possible — from noise management to community support, from property compensation to environmental protection.
card-1.num
01 — Noise Management
card-1.title
A shrinking noise footprint
card-1.body
Despite a significant increase in flights over decades, our noise contour footprint has shrunk from 825km² in 1974 to 108km² today. Our Noise Insulation Policy, respite commitments and quieter aircraft incentives will reduce it further.
card-2.num
02 — Community Impacts
card-2.title
Supporting affected communities
card-2.body
Construction will require the acquisition of 756 homes. We have planned measures to avoid, reduce, mitigate or compensate for these effects, including early acquisitions and community liaison throughout construction.
card-3.num
03 — Community Fund
card-3.title
Investing in the local area
card-3.body
A new Community Fund will help address the positive and negative effects of the project and improve quality of life. It will fund community activities, health, transport, environmental improvements and skills and jobs initiatives.
card-4.num
04 — Property & Compensation
card-4.title
Fair compensation for those affected
card-4.body
Five interim property policies cover residential property, agricultural land, commercial property, a hardship scheme and professional fees — ensuring those directly affected by land acquisition are treated fairly.
